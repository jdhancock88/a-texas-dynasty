Slideshow: Spanish Cay

Spanish Cay is a 2½-mile long island 190 miles northeast of Miami. In 1952, it was purchased by Clint Murchison Jr. It had six beaches, a storm-safe harbor, a runway, a coconut grove and a 14,000-square-foot mid-century modern house designed by Murchison. (Courtesy of the Murchison Family)

Clint Murchison Jr. built a 5,000-foot runway. His father’s DC-3, the Flying Ginny in in the foreground while Clint Jr. and his brother John’s DC-3 is to the left. (Courtesy of the Murchison family)

Clint Murchison Jr. owned a yacht named The Morning After, one of a small fleet of boats that could dock in the storm-safe harbor of Spanish Cay. (Courtesy of the Murchison Family)

Murchison Jr.’s home in Spanish Cay had floor-to-ceiling windows on all sides. The commercial-grade kitchen featured a cavernous pantry and liquor storage room. (Courtesy of the Murchison family)

The coconut palm groove on Spanish Cay was downhill from the house and was made up of hundreds of trees that reached 30 feet in height. (Courtesy of the Murchison family)

One of the six beaches on Spanish Cay. They were protected from Atlantic swells by a barrier reef a mile to the east. (Courtesy of the Murchison family)

The Point House was on the island when Clint Murchison Jr. bought it in 1952. He changed the name of the two-bedroom bungalow to the Shamrock and used it as guest quarters for his high school and college-age kids and their friends. (Courtesy of the Murchison family)
