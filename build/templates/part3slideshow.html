Slideshow: Spanish Cay

Spanish Cay is a 2½-mile long island 190 miles northeast of Miami purchased by Clint Murchison Jr. in 1952. The island sported six beaches, a storm-safe harbor, an airplane runway, a coconut grove, and a 14,000-square-foot, mid-century modern home designed by Murchison Jr. (Google Earth, © 2016 DigitalGlobe)

A 5,000-foot airstrip was built on the island to allow visitors to arrive by plane, but only during daylight hours. Runway lights were never added. (Courtesy the Murchison Family)

Clint Murchison Jr. owned a yacht named The Morning After, one of a small fleet of boats that could dock in the storm-safe harbor of Spanish Cay. (Courtesy the Murchison Family)

Murchison Jr.’s home on Spanish Cay sported floor-to-ceiling windows on all sides, a comercial-grade kitchen featuring a cavernous pantry and a large liquore storage room. (Courtesy the Murchison family)

The coconut palm groove on Spanish Cay was downhill from the house and was made up of hundreds of trees that reached 30 feet in height. (Courtesy the Murchison family)

One of the six beaches around Spanish Cay. The beaches were protected from Atlantic swells by a barrier reef a mile outshore. (Courtesy the Murchison family)
